Marquette

The Zipper’s great granddaddy on its mother’s side was a Harmony Stratotone. It’s fraternal grandparents were both (unrelated) Fender Telecaster Thinlines. Talk in town is that it’s got a great uncle that might have been a Gibson Melody Maker. It hangs out with Danelectros on the weekends. It began life as a cheap guitar approximating a vintage guitar I couldn’t afford to buy. Originally all poplar and Masonite and bargain bin hardware. Along the way I accidentally upgraded every aspect of it. It’s got a 25.5” scale length. Two volumes and one tone knob control the up and downs. You’d have to be really persuasive to get me to add another tone. I’d have to rearrange the whole room for it to fit. It strings across a Gibson style tune-o-matic and then through the body over a tailpiece I came up with. I should probably patent that thing. I feel like the string-through-edness really helps it sing. “Zip” if you will. It’s built kinda like a strategically hollowed out Oreo cookie. A top and back plate of matching wood are the cookie. The white stuff is a complementary wood with a four inch wide center block and half inch wide walls. It’s double bound for good looks and speed. At the moment we build them with a mahogany or maple neck but are looking to buck tradition in the future. I’m open to any combination of plates and body woods as well as pickup selection. Provided of course they look and sound good together. I do, after all, have to put our name on it at the end of the day.

ZIPPER SPECS

Center Block Wood Honduran/African Mahogany, Ash, Alder, Walnut, Cedar or Maple. Honestly anything that looks good and will Zip.

Top and Back Plate Wood complementary wood from the same options. I like to mix. Not always match.

Neck Wood Historically maple or Mahogany. But I’m open to suggestions.

Fingerboard Wood Indian rosewood, ebony, monterillo, pao ferro, cocobolo, maple and various other species.

Scale Length 25.5”

Body Thickness 1 13⁄16” on average.

Nut Width 1 11⁄16”

Number of frets 22

Bone nut

Bridge Nickel plated Gotoh tune-o-matic

Tailpiece Custom patent pending brushed aluminum Zipper tailpiece.

Tuners White or Silver button Klusons

Pickups We really like most everything Lollar makes so take your pick. Of course I’m always open to suggestions of other brands. Just don’t pick something atrocious.

Electronics 3 way LP style Switchcraft switch

Two volume pots and a single tone pot.

Marquette

Solid.

That was the word in mind when designing the Marquette. I wanted a simple, fundamentally solid guitar with ease of playability. And that’s exactly what the Marquette is. Full, comfortable access up and down the entire fingerboard is achieved with an offset, double-cut body style. The set neck has a full width tenon, hot hide glued into the body to achieve maximum energy transfer between the neck and body. And a wraparound bridge is used to minimize the amount of moving parts on the guitar. Thus, maximizing energy transfer from the strings to the guitar. These fundamental elements paired with Lollar pickups result in a clear, rich, high sustaining instrument.
